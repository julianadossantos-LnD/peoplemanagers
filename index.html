Leadership at StackAdapt

Culture, values, the three Leadership Principles, IC vs PM tracks, and managing yourself, your work, and your team with intention.

In this module

01 Our Culture

At StackAdapt, company culture is built through action — not through posters or policies. Every person at every level shapes the culture around them. As a fully remote and global company, maintaining culture requires intentional daily effort across digital platforms.

Observing your team: Be an impartial observer. Watch for emotions — what excites or frustrates your team? Notice what isn't there — topics unraised, channels underutilized, conversations that never happen.

StackAdapt uses CultureAmp twice per year (Q1 and Q3) to measure engagement and surface team-level insights for managers to act on.

02 The Three Leadership Principles

In addition to company values, People Managers are held to three leadership principles — non-negotiable for those in management roles.

All In

Show up with focus, resilience, and drive to achieve success regardless of obstacles.

Force Multiplier

Maximize resources, adapt to change, empower teams to achieve more. Develop, attract, and retain exceptional talent.

Champion Our Culture

Embody integrity, foster trust, create an environment where everyone feels valued and empowered.

03 IC vs. People Manager Tracks

Individual Contributor (IC)

Closest to the work. Responsible for delivering high-quality output and keeping StackAdapt running.

People Manager (PM)

Stewards of the organization. Developing people is your primary craft — not just managing tasks.

Both are assessed on Skills & Knowledge, Delivery, Quality, Care, and Adaptability — but scope and responsibility differ significantly.

04 Three Areas of Focus

Managing Yourself

Cultivate self-awareness, embrace continuous reflection, identify strengths and growth areas.

Managing the Work

Create space for great ideas, align team efforts with goals, unblock the team, drive results.

Managing the Team

Build strong relationships, leverage your manager network, practice the Leadership Principles intentionally.

01 StackAdapt's DEIB Commitment

Measured annually through Diversio surveys. Current goals: increase female + non-binary representation by 3%, BIPOC representation at all levels by 2%, increase allyship and psychological safety, and involve #superpowers-at-stack in decision-making.

02 Accommodations

Accommodations are a legal requirement — not optional. StackAdapt uses Disclo (via Okta) to manage health disclosures and accommodation requests.

When an accommodation is disclosed, you must:

  • Send a warm follow-up Slack message thanking the employee
  • Provide the Disclo link and accommodation page instructions
  • Introduce the employee to their People Business Partner (PBP)
  • Document: date, time, and what was discussed
Do NOT: ignore, dismiss, fail to document, or discourage employees from contacting their PBP.

03 Being an Equitable Leader

Inclusive leadership addresses two fundamental human needs: belonging and feeling valued.

Belonging

Create space where people feel genuinely included. Foster psychological safety every day.

Feeling Valued

Recognize that different people need different support. Equal opportunity is the floor, not the ceiling.

Authenticity

Build an environment where people can show up as their full selves every single day.

ZipHQ handles all non-T&E purchases: software renewals, new partnerships, trials, additional licenses.

All non-T&E purchases must go through ZipHQ. No exceptions.

Ivy & Gemini: Use AI to augment, not replace, human judgment. Never input StackAdapt confidential info, client data, or personal data into AI prompts.

02 Employee Wellbeing

You're often the first point of contact for wellbeing concerns. Key resources: Modern Health, EAP, Disclo.

Signs to watch for: burnout, withdrawal, cynicism, changes in sleep/eating, fatigue, feelings of ineffectiveness.

Observe with empathy — notice behavioral changes but do not diagnose. Know when to escalate to your PBP.

03 Your People Business Partner

PBPs are strategic HR partners — not just reactive support. Partner with them on performance improvement plans, terminations, accommodation requests, complex ER issues, and org changes.

Tips: Proactively share team developments. Come prepared with the issue, data, and potential solutions. Find your PBP in the PBP Contact Guide.

01 Your 4 Manager Responsibilities

Inform

Keep StackAdapters informed of workplace hazards and respond to safety concerns.

Ensure Compliance

Ensure your team completes mandatory annual H&S compliance training.

Protect

Do everything reasonable to protect from harm or work-related illness.

Lead by Example

Model healthy work-life balance, mental health awareness, and genuine self-care.

02 When an Incident Is Reported

Common remote incidents: mental health struggles/burnout, ergonomic/physical issues, reports of harassment or bullying.

  1. Use the workplace incident reporting template — document alongside the employee
  2. Escalate to your PBP if necessary
  3. Your PBP manages the investigation and advises on next steps
Key resources: Global Mental Health Benefits · Ethics Hotline · StackAdapt Health and Safety Page — all in Guru.

03 Flexible Models of Work

Work & Wander

6+ months tenure. Trips over 2 weeks need department lead approval first.

The Hub (Toronto)

Remote-first = employees cannot be penalized for not attending.

WeWork

Submit a Freshservice ticket for access. Meeting rooms should not be booked.

Relocations

4 types. All need manager + PBP + Global Mobility approval. Always partner with PBP first.

04 Taking Time Away

Leave types: Vacation, Personal Care, Culture & Belief Day, Birthday, Sick, Parental, Bereavement, Jury duty, Unpaid.

  • Show empathy — validate emotions, this may be a difficult time
  • Respect privacy — only ask what is necessary
  • Do not create guilt or imply the team will be burdened
  • For all non-vacation leave: confirm process with your PBP first
Birthday leave: Employees take their actual birthday off. Weekend birthday → preceding Friday or following Monday.

05 Employee Offboarding

Before terminating, you must be able to answer yes to all of these:

Pre-termination checklist

Have you had a direct conversation about concerns?
Have you given feedback and provided coaching?
Have you maintained detailed documentation?
Have you given verbal warnings?
Have you implemented a Reignite Plan if applicable?
Employee is NOT on job-protected leave or accommodation?
Sensitive situations: Job-protected leave (FMLA/parental/EI), active accommodation requests, employees who filed a complaint — anti-retaliation applies. Always partner with your PBP.

02 Using IOs & IDPs

Intended Outcomes (IOs) are specific, actionable outcomes written by ICs. Use them to facilitate alignment, identify growth opportunities, strengthen IDPs, and empower accountability.

Individual Development Plans (IDPs) help you maintain clear records of goals and progress, demonstrate team development, and prepare for succession planning.

03 5 Steps for Career Development Conversations

  1. Schedule quarterly — separate from performance reviews
  2. Understand current skills, strengths, and what energizes the employee
  3. Explore aspirations — give space to think big
  4. Co-create a plan with actionable steps and clear timelines
  5. Implement, review regularly, and adjust as needed
Growth ≠ people leadership. StackAdapt values both the IC track and Leadership track equally. Clarify this with your team proactively.
